Built to Find What Other Assessments Miss
Three design principles — each one closing a known gap in how health systems evaluate AI readiness
BT Compass · Methodology
Principle 01
🔍
Evidence-Gated Scoring
Self-reported confidence does not equal readiness. Every score is weighted against documentation — policies, contracts, operating artifacts, audit reports. What a health system says about itself and what their documents show are often two different numbers. BT Compass names that gap explicitly.
→ Credibility gap calculated for every domain
Principle 02
⚖️
Non-Leading Question Design
Most assessments telegraph the "right" answer. Leading questions produce flattering results. Every BT Compass question is designed to surface what's real — not what sounds good. No question implies a preferred response. Answers are calibrated on a five-level confidence scale, not yes/no.
→ Unbiased signal regardless of how respondent wants to score
Principle 03
🏗️
Domain-Neutral Reference Designs
Vendor-specific frameworks reward platform loyalty, not capability. BT Compass reference designs are built around domains — data readiness, security posture, governance maturity — not around any vendor's product roadmap. No tool earns a recommendation just by showing up.
→ Vendor recommendations based on fit, not affiliation
