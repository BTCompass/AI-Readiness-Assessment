# Demo Specification
# BT Compass AI Infrastructure Assessment
# June 19, 2026 — Bootcamp Demo Day

---

## Audience Focus

### Priority Audience
Collaborators — two segments:
1. **Healthcare validators** — health system IT leaders who will test BT Compass in a live environment to refine the methodology and establish credibility
2. **Commercialization partners** — business partners who can bring BT Compass to market as a branded and/or white-label product

Success metric: After the demo, at least one validator and one potential business partner reach out to continue the conversation.

### Key Achievement
Built a working AI infrastructure readiness assessment engine in one week — evidence-gated, domain-neutral, non-leading — that produces an honest IT findings brief and a board-ready executive scenario brief with specific, actionable next steps.

### Core Message
*Maturity models provide insight. BT Compass turns insight into action.*

---

## Narrative Architecture

### Chosen Narrative: "From Ambition to Decision"

**Rationale:** Opens with the CIO journey (relatable, immediate), shows the methodology gap without positioning against competitors, demonstrates the design philosophy, and lands on the output quality as proof. Closes with two specific asks matched to the two collaborator audiences.

**Story beats:**
- Open with a real health system CIO scenario — AI ambition, real constraints, unanswered question
- Name what's missing: maturity scores without a Monday-morning action
- Introduce the methodology: evidence-gating, non-leading questions, domain-neutral reference designs
- Walk the assessment: intake, scoring, credibility gap, honest verdict
- Show the output: IT findings brief + board-ready scenario brief
- Land the message: insight into action
- Close with two specific asks: validators and commercialization partners

---

## 3-Act Structure

### Act 1 — Setup (~1 min)
Establish the CIO's world: AI ambition, competing priorities, frozen budget authority, a board presentation in September. What he has to make decisions with vs. what he actually needs. The question nobody is answering honestly: *are we ready?*

### Act 2 — Confrontation (~3 min)
The gap between self-reported confidence and documented reality. The three design principles that close that gap. The assessment in motion — intake, 8-domain scoring, credibility gap surfacing what's backed by evidence vs. what's stated. The verdict: Not Ready. Specific, honest, no flattery.

### Act 3 — Resolution (~3 min)
The output isn't a score — it's two documents. IT findings brief for the team, board-ready scenario brief for leadership. Reusability: Pilot → Department → Enterprise, not a one-time report. Market opportunity. Two asks to close.

## Script

### Slide 1 — The CIO's World (~1 min)

"I want you to meet someone. He's a Chief Information Officer at a regional health system. Three hospitals, 5,800 employees, an active AI pilot already running with his physician group.

He's smart. He's motivated. And right now, he's sitting on a September board presentation with one question he can't fully answer: are we actually ready to invest in AI — or are we about to make a very expensive mistake?

He's not short on information. He has vendor pitches, analyst reports, maturity scores. What he doesn't have is someone who will give him an honest answer — without trying to sell him something."

---

### Slide 2 — The Methodology (~1 min)

"This is the gap BT Compass was designed to close.

Maturity models have existed in healthcare for decades. HIMSS and others have given health systems a place on a spectrum. But a score doesn't tell you what to do Monday morning. It doesn't tell you which gap is blocking you, what evidence you actually have to support your confidence, or what a realistic path forward looks like given your actual constraints.

BT Compass was built around three principles: questions that don't lead the witness, scoring that requires evidence — not just self-reported confidence — and reference designs that are domain-specific, not vendor-specific. No tool earns a recommendation just by showing up.

The result is an assessment that doesn't flatter. And that's exactly the point."

---

### Slide 3 — Assessment UX (~1 min)

"The assessment experience is designed for how health system teams actually work. Domain by domain, the CIO's team is walked through structured questions — each one designed to surface what's real, not what sounds good.

When evidence is available — a policy document, a BAA, a pilot report — the tool asks for it. When it isn't, that absence becomes part of the score.

And because we know these conversations don't always happen in one sitting, the platform tracks exactly where you are. Step away, come back, pick up where you left off. This isn't a one-afternoon exercise — it's a working session tool."

---

### Slide 4 — Scoring Output (~1 min)

"This is what the output looks like.

Eight domains. Each scored against a rubric anchored in reference designs. Each with an evidence confidence rating — because what a health system says about itself and what their documents actually show are often two different numbers. That gap has a name in BT Compass. We call it the credibility gap.

In this case, the verdict is Not Ready. Two domains in red — AI Governance and Budget Authority. Not because the health system is behind. Because those two gaps are structural, and no vendor engagement or capital commitment should happen until they're resolved.

That's an honest answer. And it's the kind of answer that builds trust with the people who have to make these decisions."

---

### Slide 5 — Scenario Brief (~1 min)

"But here's where maturity models stop and BT Compass keeps going.

The assessment doesn't end with a scorecard. It produces two deliverables. An IT findings brief for the CIO's team — every domain, every gap, every next action by role. And a board-ready scenario brief with three investment paths, a recommended direction, and a 90-day roadmap.

What you're looking at now is the actual output from a test run — built this week, using a fictional but realistic health system. Every finding specific. Every recommendation tied to real constraints.

Maturity models provide insight. BT Compass turns insight into action."

---

### Slide 6 — Active Tool (~30 sec)

"And this isn't a one-time engagement.

A $5,000 assessment that gets revisited in three years isn't a tool — it's a report. BT Compass is designed to travel with the organization. Pilot readiness. Department-level expansion. Enterprise-wide foundation. The same methodology, applied at whatever scope the decision requires, as often as the situation changes."

---

### Slide 7 — Market Sizing (~30 sec)

"The addressable market for a product that does this honestly is significant. Six thousand eight hundred hospitals in the United States. Thousands of primary care and specialty practices — essentially any organization running an EHR and asking the question every health system is asking right now: are we ready for AI, and how do we know?"

---

### Slide 8 — How to Engage (~1 min)

"I'm here today looking for two kinds of collaborators.

First — validators. Health system IT leaders who want to run a live assessment in their environment, stress-test the methodology against real data, and help refine the product into something the industry can trust. If you've sat in that CIO chair and felt the gap between what a maturity model tells you and what you actually need to know — I'd like to talk.

Second — commercialization partners. People who see the market opportunity here and want to bring BT Compass to market — as a branded product, a white-label solution, or both.

The product works. The methodology is sound. What comes next is validating it in the real world and getting it to the people who need it.

Thank you."
